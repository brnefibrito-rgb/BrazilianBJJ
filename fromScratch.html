Guard Passing

The art of breaking through the bottom game

Why Guard Passing Matters

Guard passing is the central skill that divides recreational grapplers from serious competitors. Your opponent's guard is a weapon — long legs, active grips, mobile hips — and learning to dismantle it systematically is learning to think under pressure.

Every great pass is built on posture, weight distribution, and patience. Before you can move around or through the guard, you must break your opponent's connection to you. That starts with grips, and grips start with understanding where your own base lives.

Core Passing Systems

  • Pressure Passing
    1. Stack the hips to eliminate shrimping
    2. Drive your shoulder into their midsection
    3. Cut the knee across and settle into side control
    4. Hold for 3 full seconds before transitioning
  • Torreando (Bullfighter)
    1. Control both ankles with a strong grip
    2. Push the legs to one side simultaneously
    3. Drive your knee to the mat and step around
    4. Flatten them before they can re-guard
  • Over-Under Pass
    1. Thread one arm under the leg, one arm over
    2. Lock your hands behind the far hip
    3. Drive forward and stack their weight onto their shoulders
    4. Walk your body perpendicular to finish
